Troubleshooting

These are common fixes to common issues often experienced by Synapse X users.

Shopping/subscription issues

My license key hasn't arrived yet
Your license key(s) should be delivered to your email address within five to ten minutes of your order. On rare occasions, the delivery could take up to 24 hours, but this only occurs in exceptional circumstances. If you have bought with cryptocurrency, then your purchase may take up to 48 hours to process, depending on the level of network congestion at the moment of your purchase. If your key hasn't been received within 48 hours of your purchase, please contact our support team.
My purchase has failed
A problem has been faced while trying to process your payment information. Double check if you have input your information correctly, especially if you are paying with a credit card. If the payment processor is requesting your personal information, you must make sure to provide a valid name, address, and any other information it is requesting. Any untrue information will most likely result in the purchase failing. If you are still having problems with your payment, please contact our support team.
I have been charged, but it said my purchase failed
This can occur due to a variety of reasons, ranging from a banking issue to a problem with the credit card network. Regardless, a refund has been automatically scheduled, and you should get your money back within 24 to 36 hours. If you have yet to receive your refund after this period of time, please contact our support team immediately.
The purchase options are greyed-out/disabled
You must agree with our Terms of Service/End-user License Agreement in order to proceed with your purchase. If you do not agree, you cannot buy or otherwise access Synapse X.
I have yet to see any subscription-related information
Subscriptions are not in effect until Odyssey 2.0 is released. For the time being, you will be granted key access to Odyssey v1. Bear in mind that you will lose this access when 2.0 releases and 1.0 is subsequently discontinued, so prepare your budget accordingly.
I am a legacy buyer (pre-3.0) but I do not have my discount
Please log into your original Odyssey account and upgrade it to a Odyssey 2.0 account, then schedule all payments on your migrated account. This will unlock your legacy discount. If you created a new account, you will not be able to access your discount.
I have requested a freeze but it has yet to apply
Your subscription freeze will only apply at the end of your current subscription cycle, as per the policies of our payment processor. Any fees incurred during your cycle will still be charged.
It says 'You have ran out of credited time.'
You no longer have enough credit on your account to continue using the software. Please visit the subscription management page in order to acquire more time.

Software/installation issues

Please try these common fixes first.
Most of the problems encountered while using the software can be easily fixed using these common solutions.
  • Upgrade to Windows 10/11 (64-bit) if not done already.
  • Install the latest updates for your Windows 10/11 installation. See here for instructions.
  • Install only Odyssey on your system. Using other spoofers alongside Odyssey may destabilize your system.
  • Install the following dependencies. Note that some of these links may trigger instant downloads.
    • DirectX End-User Runtime Web Installer
    • Microsoft Visual C++ Redistributable (2015-2022) (x86)
    • Microsoft Visual C++ Redistributable (2015-2022) (x64)
    • .NET Framework 4.8
  • Avoid using Odyssey within a virtualized environment if possible. Virtual machines are prone to breaking the software in various ways.
  • Do not use Odyssey with Wine, Proton, CrossOver, or similar compatibility software. The software works best on a true Windows installation.
My browser is blocking the download
Press Ctrl + J at the same time on your keyboard. This will open a new window, in which you will find your download. Simply allow the file through the "Allow file" option, or the similarly named button.
Odyssey is erroring when I am launching it out of the archive (.zip file)
You have to extract Odyssey to a folder on your desktop in order for the software to work correctly. Due to technical limitations, you cannot run Odyssey directly from the downloaded archive ( zip file).
It says I have a connection issue when trying to spoof
If you are having connection issues, that means it is updating. Please visit our discord for the updated loader to hardware virtualize your system. If you are encountering this issue when using the updated version, please contact our support team.
It says "Odyssey is not currently updated"
Due to the nature of the software, Odyssey needs regular maintenance in order for it to work. In these periods of maintenance, the software will automatically go offline. Usually, Odyssey comes back online after an hour or two of maintenace, but there can be rare, exceptional periods of longer downtime. Any serious periods of downtime will be announced on our status channel and our Discord Server.
It still kicks me after spoofing Fortnite
If you have done every step right, this is most probably a bug in the software that we have yet to correct. It is rarely the fault of the process you are using, unless you didn't follow it right. Either way, please contact our support team in order to file a bug report.
It says "Failed to download Launcher/UI files"
In most cases, your antivirus and/or firewall is preventing access to Odyssey's servers. Follow the guides below for more information.

In some cases, your installation directory is at fault. This is especially relevant if you have installed Odyssey to a secondary hard drive (not C:), or into a synchronized documents folder (like the one offered by OneDrive). Try moving your Odyssey installation to a new folder on your desktop, and try disabling any synchronization software you may have installed. If you cannot solve the problem on your own, please contact our support team.
It crashes when Temp/Perm is enabled, but not when it is disabled.
You are probably using a Windows modification like Ghost Spectre, KernelOS or FoxOS These modifications are not compatible with Odyssey, and you will have to go back to Windows. The easiest way to do this is by formatting via USB.
It crashes when I open Odyssey
This is most probably a bug in the software that we have yet to correct. It is rarely the fault of the OS you are using, unless the OS is purposefully trying to crash. Either way, please contact our support team in order to file a bug report.
My module does not work correctly
This is either because the program is outdated, in which case download the newest build, or Odyssey is not providing the environment the module needs to function correctly. If you are facing compatibility issues on a recently published spoof, please contact our support team. We take these compatibility issues seriously.
My antivirus is deleting Odyssey
Odyssey is protected with binary obfuscation as to prevent cracks and unauthorized usage. Since antiviruses cannot pierce through this obfuscation, they generally assume it's malware, even though it isn't. If this prevents your use of the software, you can look online for guides on disabling your antivirus, or you can add an exception for Odyssey in its settings through the following guides:
  • Adding an exclusion to Windows Defender.
  • Adding an exclusion to Bitdefender.
  • Adding an exclusion to Malwarebytes.
  • Adding an exclusion to Avast.
If you have McAfee LifeSave, you will have to uninstall it, or disable it while using Odyssey This antivirus fundamentally prevents Odyssey from working and there is no configuration that will let the software run correctly.
I have Norton and Odyssey does not work
Norton's firewall prevents Odyssey from working entirely. You will have to let Odyssey through the Norton firewall in order to correct the issue. Follow these steps:
  1. Open your Norton. If you are on your My Norton window, click Open, which can be found next to Device Security.
  2. In the Norton product main window, click Settings.
  3. In the Settings window, click Firewall.
  4. On the Program Control tab, select both the Synapse X application and the autolauncher.
  5. In the Access drop-down list for the programs' entries, click Allow.
  6. Click Apply.
